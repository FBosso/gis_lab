Conclusion

The intercomparison and accuracy analysis of the WorldPop layer and the GHSL revealed the WorldPop layer to generally be of higher accuracy with an overall accuracy of 67.2%. Additionally, its reliability of classes that can be found as such on the ground (user’s accuracy) together with the probability of a class on the ground to be correctly identified on the map (producer’s accuracy) are both significantly higher in the WorldPop layer than in the GHSL. The intercomparison of the two rasters before reclassification to built and not built areas revealed a high similarity in population values, with a mean of differences of -0.33 (the difference is zero when the maps are equal), suggesting a close model to reality. However, the intercomparison furthermore exposed a zone in the study area where population values between the two maps differed significantly, emphasizing that census data may fail to closely represent reality within this area and should thus be handled with care.
